Why do I paint

Every painting is an journey, a solo trip, both adventurous and calming. As soon as I start painting, I stop thinking about everything else - my surroundings, my pains, my past, and my future. Every single painting of mine is a product of both planning and accident. Half way through every painting, I feel I am going to spoil it - a fall into a deep abyss. Then I watch myself rising from that abyss and heading towards my destination. No matter how hard I plan, there is some sort of surprise waiting for me in each painting. The process is what I enjoy more than the end result. Yes, painting is the ultimate getaway for me from my reality - a meditation I love to be engaged in again and again.
